African Elephant, the Overcompensating Mammal.
    Elephants use their trunk for smelling, breathing, detecting vibrations, caressing their young, sucking up 
    water, and grasping objects. The tip of their trunk is comprised of two opposable extensions, or fingers, 
    which allow for extreme dexterity.
    An African elephant’s large ears also serve many purposes. The ears’ large surface area helps radiate 
    excess heat under the harsh African sun. The ears are also often used to communicate visually. 
    Flapping their ears can signify either aggression or joy.
