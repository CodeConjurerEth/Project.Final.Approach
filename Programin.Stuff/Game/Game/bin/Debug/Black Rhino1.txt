Black Rhino, is there a Hispanic one too?
    The black rhinos live in the African Savanna, and since they’re endangered they can’t really live in groups.
    Except for the females and their offspring, the male rhinos are solitary, living the bachelor life as they
    please. Once mature, meaning about 3 years the calves have to start living alone.
    They feed during the night and early morning, they can eat in evening as well.
    To cover from the sun and to protect themselves, black rhinos roll in the mud. Hence the sun is blocked and 
    they can cool off.
    Their diet consists mainly of plants, but they are browsers, they prefer trees and bushes to ground plants.
